Giving Actionable Feedback:

Developing effective leadership through actionable feedback: Building trust, engagement, and growth

February 14th, 2023

What is actionable feedback?

Actionable feedback allows people leaders to identify issues important to their teams and the organization - it's not complaining, but rather it allows an action with disposition of feedback in one of three ways: I heard you and changed something, I heard you but decided not to change something, or I heard you and would like to change something but can't at this time for these reasons.

You as a leader...

Actionable feedback requires that you develop a followership through loyalty, humility, skill sets, and honesty:

Loyalty requires that teams and people leaders agree to the way ahead after dialog and will notbad-mouth or undermine the agreed upon plan - the team and people leader are a unified front.

Humility requires that people leaders realize that they do not know everything and need their people to provide actionable feedback - ignoring this opportunity will render them ineffective and soon without followers.

Skill sets means that people leaders need to be the subject matter experts and know what they are talking about to build credibility and trust.

Honesty - there is nothing worse than being surrounded by unauthentic people and bosses.

  • Tell them what they need to hear as opposed to what they want to hear

  • Actionable feedback demonstrates employee engagement

  • People leaders grow themselves and their people through actionable feedback - it builds trust as a cultural cornerstone

  • Be specific, don't use it to accuse others, and finally offer potential solutions when giving actionable feedback
